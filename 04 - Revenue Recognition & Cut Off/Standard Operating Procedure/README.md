## Overview
This directory contains the official Standard Operating Procedure governing how revenue is recognised and allocated to the correct accounting period.

The objective is to ensure revenue is:

- accurate  
- complete  
- compliant with IFRS 15  
- recorded in the proper reporting period  
- fully supportable for audit  

Revenue is typically one of the highest risk and most scrutinised areas of the financial statements.

---

## What This SOP Covers
The procedure defines mandatory controls for:

- Contract and performance obligation assessment  
- Determination of transaction price  
- Timing of revenue recognition  
- Month-end cut-off testing  
- Deferred and unbilled revenue  
- Reconciliations to the general ledger  
- Analytical review expectations  
- Documentation standards  

It applies to **all revenue streams**, **all customer arrangements**, and **all reporting periods**.

## Core Control Principles Embedded in This SOP

### 1) IFRS 15 Compliance
Revenue is recognised when performance obligations are satisfied.

### 2) Evidence of Delivery
No recognition without proof of service or goods transferred.

### 3) Period Accuracy
Revenue must fall in the correct reporting month.

### 4) Independent Oversight
Finance management review and approval is mandatory.

## Month-End Cut-Off Focus Areas
During close, particular attention is required for:

- Services delivered but not yet invoiced  
- Deferred income releases  
- Invoice vs delivery date mismatches  
- Manual revenue adjustments  
- Trend or margin anomalies
