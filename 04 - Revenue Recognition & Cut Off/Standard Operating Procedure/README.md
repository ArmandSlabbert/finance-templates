## Overview
This directory contains the official Standard Operating Procedure governing how revenue is recognised and recorded in the correct accounting period.

The objective is to ensure revenue is:

- accurate  
- complete  
- valid  
- compliant with IFRS 15  
- supported by clear evidence  

Revenue is one of the highest-risk areas in financial reporting and receives significant audit scrutiny.

---

## What This SOP Covers
The procedure establishes mandatory controls for:

- Application of IFRS 15 principles  
- Contract and performance obligation assessment  
- Determination of transaction prices  
- Timing of revenue recognition  
- Month-end cut-off testing  
- Treatment of unbilled and deferred revenue  
- Reconciliation and analytical review  
- Documentation and retention  

It applies to **all revenue streams, contracts, and billing activities**.

## Control Principles Embedded in This SOP

### 1) IFRS Compliance
Revenue must follow IFRS 15 requirements.

### 2) Evidence of Performance
Recognition requires proof of delivery or service completion.

### 3) Contract Clarity
Performance obligations and pricing must be documented.

### 4) Period Accuracy
Revenue belongs in the period earned â€” not invoiced.

### 5) Independent Oversight
Finance Manager review and sign-off required.

## Core Activities in This SOP

### Contract & Policy Governance
- Maintain approved revenue policy  
- Review contracts for obligations  

### Recognition Rules
- Recognise revenue when obligations are satisfied  
- Retain delivery/service evidence  

### Month-End Cut-Off
- Test transactions around period end  
- Accrue unbilled revenue  
- release deferred revenue correctly  
- align invoices with completion dates  

### Reconciliation & Analytics
- Tie sub-ledgers to GL  
- perform trend analysis  
- investigate anomalies
