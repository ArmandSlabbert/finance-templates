# SOP – Balance Sheet Reconciliations

## Overview
This folder contains the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) and
supporting tools governing the preparation, review, approval, and
documentation of balance sheet reconciliations.

The objective is to ensure all balance sheet accounts are complete,
accurate, fully supported, and reconciled on a timely basis, preserving
the integrity of the financial statements and maintaining audit
readiness.

## Contents

### SOP
• **SOP_Balance_Sheet_Reconciliations.docx**  
Defines the policies, controls, and responsibilities for identifying,
preparing, reviewing, approving, and retaining balance sheet
reconciliations.

### Operational Checklist
• **Balance_Sheet_Reconciliation_Checklist.xlsx**  
A structured reconciliation tracker used to monitor completion status,
supporting documentation, review sign-off, and exception follow-ups for
each reporting period.

## Key Areas Covered
• Identification of balance sheet accounts requiring reconciliation  
• Monthly reconciliation requirements and timelines  
• Opening balance validation  
• Movement analysis and reasonableness checks  
• Reconciliation to supporting schedules and third-party statements  
• Ageing and long-outstanding item review  
• Exception identification and resolution  
• Review, approval, and segregation of duties  
• Audit trail and document retention standards  

## How This Is Used
The SOP establishes the control framework for balance sheet
reconciliations, while the checklist is used operationally during each
reporting cycle to track reconciliation completion, review, and approval.

Completed reconciliations and checklists are retained as part of the
month-end close documentation and audit support files.

## Outputs
• Fully reconciled balance sheet accounts  
• Documented explanations for reconciling items  
• Signed-off reconciliation checklist for the reporting period  

## Standards
IFRS – Accrual Basis of Accounting  
IAS 1 – Presentation of Financial Statements
