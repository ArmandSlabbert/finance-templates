## Overview
This directory contains the official Standard Operating Procedure governing journal entries processed after an accounting period has been closed.

The objective is to ensure post-close changes are:

- valid  
- authorised  
- transparent  
- fully supported  
- audit defensible  

Once a period is closed, financial integrity becomes paramount.

---

## What This SOP Covers
The procedure establishes mandatory controls for:

- Identification of errors and omissions  
- Materiality assessment  
- Reclassification requirements  
- Correction vs estimate judgement  
- Approval hierarchy  
- Audit trail maintenance  
- Reporting and disclosure considerations  

It applies to **all journals processed after formal period close**, including audit adjustments.

## Control Principles Embedded in This SOP

### 1) Closed Means Controlled
Post-close activity must be exceptional, not routine.

### 2) Approval Before Posting
Finance Manager authorisation is mandatory.

### 3) Full Transparency
Every adjustment must be explainable to auditors.

### 4) No Hidden Profit Movement
Reclasses should not affect results unless correcting errors.

### 5) Logged & Traceable
A register of changes must exist.

---

## Core Activities in This SOP

### Identification
- detect errors or misclassifications  
- determine materiality  
- evaluate whether reopening is necessary  

### Reclassification
- move balances to correct accounts  
- document rationale  
- protect reported totals where appropriate  

### Error Corrections
- distinguish from estimate updates  
- assess impact on comparatives  

### Approval & Governance
- Finance Manager review  
- attach support  
- record in post-close register  

### Reporting Considerations
- evaluate disclosure impact  
- communicate significant items to management
